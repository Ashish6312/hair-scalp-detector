Telogen Effluvium

A detailed guide to understanding this common form of temporary, stress-induced hair shedding and the path to recovery.

Common Symptoms

Increased Hair Shedding

A noticeable increase in hair falling out during washing, brushing, or on your pillow.

Diffuse Thinning

Hair loss occurs evenly across the entire scalp rather than in specific patches or spots.

Delayed Onset

Hair shedding typically begins about 2 to 4 months after a specific triggering event.

No Scalp Inflammation

The scalp itself usually appears healthy, without the redness, itching, or scaling seen in other conditions.

Treatment Options

Address the Trigger

The most effective treatment is identifying and resolving the underlying cause, such as stress or a nutritional deficiency.

Nutritional Support

Ensuring a balanced diet rich in iron, zinc, protein, and vitamins to support healthy hair growth.

Gentle Hair Care

Avoiding harsh chemical treatments and tight hairstyles to minimize further stress on the hair follicles.

Stress Management

Incorporating techniques like meditation, yoga, and regular exercise to manage stress levels.

Management Tips

Be Patient

Recognize that hair regrowth is a slow process and it can take several months for shedding to normalize.

Check Medications

Review any medications with your doctor, as some can contribute to this type of hair shedding.

Seek Professional Advice

Consult a dermatologist to confirm the diagnosis and rule out other forms of hair loss.

Detailed Information

What is Telogen Effluvium?

Resting Phase Shift: It occurs when a significant stressor pushes a large number of hair follicles into the resting (telogen) phase simultaneously.

Shedding Phase: A few months later, these hairs are shed, leading to noticeable but temporary thinning.

Reversible: Unlike pattern baldness, it is almost always reversible once the trigger is removed and the hair cycle normalizes.

Common Triggers

Major Stress: Significant emotional or physiological stress from events like surgery, high fever, or psychological trauma.

Childbirth: Postpartum hair loss is a very common form of Telogen Effluvium.

Nutritional Deficiencies: Lack of iron, zinc, protein, or essential fatty acids.

Sudden Weight Loss: Crash dieting can be a significant shock to the system, triggering shedding.
