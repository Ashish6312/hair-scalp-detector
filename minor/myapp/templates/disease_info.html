Types of Hair & Scalp Diseases

A comprehensive guide to common hair and scalp conditions, with detailed information and treatment insights.

Folliculitis

Inflammation of hair follicles causing red, swollen bumps. Often caused by bacterial or fungal infections.

Read More

Contact Dermatitis

An allergic reaction or irritation from substances like dyes or shampoos, causing a red, itchy rash on the scalp.

Read More

Alopecia

Hair loss condition resulting in bald patches. Can be caused by genetics, stress, or autoimmune factors.

Read More

Psoriasis

Autoimmune condition causing red, scaly patches on the scalp. Often accompanied by thick, silvery scales.

Read More

Seborrheic Dermatitis

Inflammatory skin condition causing scaly, itchy rash. Common in oily areas of the scalp and face.

Read More

Head Lice

Parasitic insects living on the scalp that cause intense itching and irritation. Spreads easily through close contact.

Read More

Lichen Planus

An inflammatory condition causing itchy, purple-red bumps or patches on the scalp, sometimes leading to permanent hair loss.

Read More

Male Pattern Baldness

The most common type of hair loss in men, characterized by a receding hairline and thinning at the crown.

Read More

Telogen Effluvium

A temporary form of widespread hair shedding, often triggered by stress, illness, or hormonal changes.

Read More

Tinea Capitis

Also known as scalp ringworm, this is a contagious fungal infection causing itchy, scaly, bald patches.
